# Vaulted — 20-Minute VC Call · Prep Script

**Purpose:** Everything you need for the 20-minute call from the moment
Julia (or whoever) accepts to the moment you send the follow-up email.

**Rule of the doc:** print pages 1–3 and keep them in your lap. Memorise
nothing. Do not read from the script during the call — read *before*
it, then leave the room mentally clean.

---

## 📋 THE 20-MINUTE TIMELINE (target)

```
  0:00 ─ 0:02   Small talk / calibration
  0:02 ─ 0:05   Frame the call · ask what they want
  0:05 ─ 0:12   The condensed pitch (7 min max)
  0:12 ─ 0:17   Their questions · your deep answers
  0:17 ─ 0:19   Their concerns · your calm handling
  0:19 ─ 0:20   The close · specific next step
```

**Rule of thumb:** if by minute 15 you are still on the pitch, you've
lost. VCs care more about how you *think* than what you've built. The
Q&A is where the meeting is won or lost. Compress the pitch. Expand
the dialogue.

## 🎯 PRE-CALL PREP (24 hours before)

### Research

Spend 45 minutes on Julia specifically. Not the fund — the human.

- [ ] Read her last 3 LinkedIn posts. Note anything she references.
- [ ] Search "Julia Hawkins LocalGlobe" on YouTube — she's spoken at
      Slush, LSE, and on the 20VC podcast. Watch 10 minutes.
- [ ] Read LocalGlobe's last 3 investment announcements. Pattern-match:
      what stage, what geography, what thesis?
- [ ] Check her Twitter/X — look for hot-take patterns. Does she believe
      in stablecoins? Wise? UK fintech in general?
- [ ] Find one specific thing she's said publicly you can reference
      without name-dropping (see "The Calibration Move" below).

### Warm-up materials

- [ ] Have the deck open in a browser tab, ready to screen-share
- [ ] Have the Loom link on your clipboard (in case she asks to see the
      demo without screen-sharing)
- [ ] Have your **phone in your hand** with the app open on the Send
      Money screen — if the video call is on your laptop, you can pick
      up the phone and film it doing the actual send. This is the
      power move most founders don't think of.
- [ ] Have a friend's real KYC-verified test-account phone number ready
      to receive a real £5 send during the call if she asks
- [ ] Notes app or Notion open with the "likely questions" cheat sheet
      (below in §7) minimised — glance-able, not visible

### Physical setup

- [ ] Same rules as the Loom setup: eye-level camera, front-lit face,
      quiet room, headphones in
- [ ] Backup wifi (mobile hotspot ready) — a dropped call kills momentum
- [ ] Water within reach (not coffee — you'll get jittery)
- [ ] Second monitor with the notes; primary monitor with her face
- [ ] Close every other browser tab — a Slack notification popping
      mid-pitch is amateur hour

### Mental prep (30 minutes before)

- [ ] Walk around the block. Do NOT rehearse the pitch in the last hour.
      Over-rehearsed pitches sound rehearsed. Cold pitches sound like
      thinking.
- [ ] Remind yourself: **she has three follow-on meetings today**. You're
      allowed to be the interesting one. Don't perform.
- [ ] One-line internal mantra: "*I'm here to see if this is a fit —
      both ways.*" This mantra is doing work — it flips the frame from
      "please invest in me" to "let's find out."

## 🎬 THE FIRST TWO MINUTES (0:00–0:02)

The first two minutes decide the tone of the whole call. Get them
wrong and you spend the next 18 minutes clawing back.

### Opening (word-for-word)

> "Hi Julia, thanks so much for making the time. Before I dive in,
> is now still good — do we have the full twenty minutes, or should
> I compress?"

**Why this works:**
- Signals you respect her calendar (VCs are calendar-hostage all day)
- Forces her to explicitly reaffirm 20 min, which she'll then honour
- Prevents her from cutting you off at 12 min because she double-booked
- Signals seniority — junior founders never ask this

**Her likely response:** "Yeah, we have twenty. Great to meet you."

Then, the calibration move:

### The calibration move (30 seconds)

> "Perfect. I've been really looking forward to this — I noticed on
> [reference the specific thing you found in research] that you've
> been thinking about [specific theme, e.g. 'the compression of
> banking-rail costs' / 'diaspora fintech' / 'unbundling incumbent
> spreads']. What I'm building is directly in that lane — is it
> useful if I give you the two-minute overview and then let you
> steer, or would you rather I just start with the demo?"

**Why this works:**
- Proves you did homework — 10× reply-forward rate on the follow-up email
- Gives HER the choice of what she sees first — human beings always
  invest more attention in a path they chose
- Anchors YOU as the one giving her options, not begging for time

**Her likely response:**
- 60%: "Give me the overview." → proceed to condensed pitch (§4)
- 30%: "Just show me the demo." → jump to demo (§5), pitch after
- 10%: "Tell me first — how did you and your co-founder meet?" → this
       is a HUGE positive signal. Answer authentically, THEN pivot.

## 🎯 THE CONDENSED PITCH — 7 MINUTES (0:02–0:12)

You have Appendix B of `/app/docs/INVESTOR_PITCH.md` as the 15-min
long-form pitch. Below is the compressed 7-minute version.

Do NOT read this aloud. Talk to her.

### Beat 1 · The problem (60 seconds)

> "The UK diaspora sends £13 billion home a year — Kenya, Nigeria,
> Ghana, India, Philippines. Banks and Western Union take about £980
> million of it in fees. Seven to nine percent on the UK-Africa
> corridors specifically. There's a Kenyan NHS nurse in Peckham
> paying £22 every month to Wise, and that's a term of her nephew's
> school fees. The rails are broken and everyone knows it."

*[Pause. Let this land.]*

### Beat 2 · The insight (60 seconds)

> "Here's the thing nobody's shipped yet: crypto rails already solve
> this. USDC on Polygon settles in two seconds at under a penny.
> Stellar and XRP are similar. The rails are 1,000× cheaper than SWIFT.
> But every crypto app in the world has forced the user to actually
> understand what a chain is. And every remittance app in the world
> has kept custody of the money — which is why they still need a
> banking licence in every corridor, and why they still hit the
> correspondent-bank fee floor."

*[This is where you win skeptics. Slow down.]*

### Beat 3 · The solution + demo (2 minutes)

> "Vaulted is the first app where the entire user experience is
> fiat — you type pounds, tap a flag, tap Send — and the whole
> stablecoin routing happens invisibly, on-device signed with the
> user's own key. Let me show you."
>
> *[Screen-share the app OR pick up your phone. Send a real £5 to
> whichever friend or team member you pre-arranged. Narrate as you go:]*
>
> "So I'm typing 5 pounds… I pick Kenya… you can see the live quote:
> 5 pounds becomes 873 shillings, fee 6 pence, arriving in about five
> seconds… Face ID… and — [wait 3-5 seconds for confirmation] — done.
> That's the on-chain transaction on Polygonscan if you want to see
> it, and here's the audit log for our compliance record."

**Do the send WHILE talking.** The best VC pitches contain a moment of
"oh wow, that just actually happened."

### Beat 4 · Why we win (90 seconds)

> "Three things make this defensible. One: chain gas as our fee floor
> trends down every quarter. Their bank-rail floor doesn't. Time
> compounds in our favour. Two: because we're self-custody, we don't
> need custody licences in every corridor country — we're a CEP
> under UK MLR 2017, which is a much lighter lift than the MTL Wise
> needs. Three: our economics are structurally cleaner because we
> don't earn on FX spread widening — we earn on send volume. So we
> can compress fees and out-compete them without cannibalising our
> own revenue base."

### Beat 5 · Where we are (60 seconds)

> "We're not a slide-deck company. Vaulted is live in production.
> Six chains, eight corridors, Stripe in Live mode for both
> subscriptions and Identity KYC, sanctions screening via
> OpenSanctions, MLR-2017-standard audit log, 53 automated tests.
> Referral loop and encrypted chat both shipped. Everything you'd
> expect for an FCA-track fintech we've either shipped or spec'd."

### Beat 6 · The ask (30 seconds)

> "We're raising 1.2 million pound seed on a 6 million pre-money
> SAFE. Eighteen-month runway. Forty percent goes into the FCA
> money-transmission licence and compliance hire. Thirty percent
> into two senior engineers. Twenty into growth. Ten into runway.
> Base case: 25,000 monthly actives and 8 million pounds monthly
> transaction volume by month 18. Series A opens at month 15."

*[Full stop. Then:]*

> "What questions do you have?"

**Absolutely do NOT keep talking after "What questions do you have?"**
The silence that follows is uncomfortable. Sit in it. She will speak
first. If she doesn't within 6 seconds, add: "Or I can walk you
through the deck?"

## 💬 Q&A HANDLING (0:12–0:19)

This is where the meeting is actually won.

### The three universal rules

1. **Answer the question that was asked, not the question you
   wanted.** VCs test for this. If she asks "what happens if USDC
   de-pegs" and you pivot to "let me tell you about our roadmap",
   she registers it as evasion.

2. **"I don't know" is a valid answer.** If you don't know, say so,
   commit to a follow-up. E.g.: "I don't have that number in front
   of me — I'll send it in the follow-up email tonight." Then
   actually do it. This is a 10× credibility move.

3. **Never disagree by contradiction. Disagree by re-framing.**
   Wrong: "That's not right, actually…"
   Right: "That's a fair concern. The way we think about it is…"

### Reading the room

**Signals she's interested:**
- Asks about specific corridors ("what's your GHS margin?")
- Asks about you personally ("what were you doing before?")
- Asks about the round ("who else are you talking to?")
- Notes something on paper
- Extends the call past 20 minutes

**Signals she's checked out:**
- Non-specific "interesting" reactions
- Camera drift (looking off-screen at another monitor)
- Asks about "the crypto piece" repeatedly (may mean she has an
  internal LP fund-mandate issue)
- Says "we tend to invest at Series A, but keep me updated"

**When you spot disengagement:** shorten your answers, ask HER a
question. Example: "That's the framework — but I'm curious, what's
LocalGlobe's current view on stablecoin-native fintech?" A good VC
will engage; a bad match will politely deflect. Either way you learn.

## 🧠 CHEAT SHEET · THE 12 MOST LIKELY QUESTIONS

Print this. Keep it glance-able.

### 1. "Why hasn't Wise done this yet?"

> "They can't without cannibalising their custodial float, which is
> a material part of their P&L. Their board would fight it. Meanwhile
> we have a 24-month head start and community lock-in."

### 2. "What happens if USDC de-pegs?"

> "Multichain routing. We can settle on Stellar, XRP, or Bitcoin as
> fallbacks in the same product. We'd pause USDC corridors for a
> few hours and route through XLM until the peg restores. It's
> already coded — we route for cost today, but the same router can
> route for peg stability."

### 3. "How defensible is this against a Revolut or Monzo launch?"

> "Two things. First, they'd need to build the multi-chain custody
> stack, which is 6-12 months of engineering they haven't scoped.
> Second, they'd need to convince their board that self-custody
> doesn't cannibalise their card and interchange revenue — which it
> does. Big fintechs are structurally slow on stablecoin because
> their own cap tables punish them for shipping it."

### 4. "What's your CAC assumption based on?"

> "Two data points. One, Remitly's blended CAC in year two was £4.10
> — and their message was 'faster transfers'; ours is '80% cheaper'.
> Two, empirical for money-saving fintech to diaspora communities on
> TikTok is £2.20-£2.60. We modelled £3.20 to be conservative."

### 5. "Why UK-first? Why not launch broader?"

> "Regulatory clarity, corridor economics, and diaspora density.
> The FCA regime is well-understood, MTL is achievable in 12-18
> months. UK-outbound corridors to Africa are among the highest-fee
> in the world, so our savings message is loudest here. And 3.2m
> sub-Saharan Africans in the UK live in three cities — the
> referral loop has the highest velocity here of anywhere in Europe."

### 6. "What's your dilution ceiling? Are you willing to accept X on Series A?"

> "Target 18-22% at Series A on £30M+ pre. That's the plan. Willing
> to flex a few points if the strategic value of the lead is
> meaningfully differentiated — but we're not looking to give up
> control."

### 7. "How real is the FCA path?"

> "We've built to MLR 2017 standard from day one — audit log,
> sanctions screening, KYC via Stripe Identity, tier limits with
> hard blocks. The MLRO hire is one of our first post-close moves.
> The application itself is a 4-6 month process. Base case: MTL
> granted by month 15. Worst case: sandbox pathway as fallback,
> which extends timeline by 6 months but doesn't kill the business."

### 8. "Who else are you talking to?"

> "Passion, Ada, Kindred, and a couple of angels who've been early
> at Wise and Monzo. Nobody's committed yet — I wanted to talk to
> LocalGlobe first because your Wise pattern-matching is unique."

*[Truth is your ally here — don't invent competitive pressure that
doesn't exist. If she smells a lie, the round is dead.]*

### 9. "What's the biggest risk?"

> "FCA MTL rejection. It's why 40% of the raise goes to compliance —
> we're front-loading that risk. Second biggest: chain gas becoming
> uneconomical, but the trend is the opposite. Third: user
> onboarding friction — solved by hiding the crypto, but there's
> still a KYC drop-off we're actively optimising."

*[Naming your own risks is the ultimate credibility move. Never
pretend nothing worries you — a VC who believes you have no worries
believes you haven't thought hard.]*

### 10. "What's the exit story? Who buys you?"

> "Two paths. One: an IPO in the Wise mould at £2-8bn cap, six to
> eight years out. Two: strategic acquisition by a large fintech
> looking to enter self-custody — Revolut, Monzo, Wise itself,
> possibly Coinbase or Kraken looking to move down-market into
> mainstream users. But I'd like to build to IPO. Not chasing an
> exit."

### 11. "Why should I back this team over an ex-Revolut / ex-Wise team?"

> "Two reasons. First, we've already shipped what an ex-Revolut
> team is still pitching. Speed of shipping is the moat here, and
> we've proven it. Second, we ARE the user — I'm [context: I sent
> home to my family / my co-founder grew up in the community, etc.].
> The ex-Revolut team has domain expertise. We have user empathy.
> Both matter but at this stage the second matters more."

### 12. "What would need to be true for you to raise 2M instead of 1.2M?"

*[This is a positive signal — she's mentally sizing up.]*

> "If we had FCA MTL confidence-of-grant already, we'd raise 2 million
> and go harder on growth. Without it, more capital is just extended
> burn. So the plan is: 1.2 now, hit MTL, then Series A at 30M+ pre
> around month 15-18. Happy to revisit if you'd want to lead a
> bigger cheque."

## 🔥 HANDLING HARD PUSHBACK (the moments the meeting shifts)

### Pushback A: "I don't understand why anyone would use this over Wise."

**Wrong response:** "Well, actually…"

**Right response (calm, curious):**
> "Fair question. Can I ask — when you send money internationally
> yourself, what do you use? [Wait for answer.] And what do you
> actually think about the fees? [Wait.] Right — so imagine that
> fee halving, and the money arriving in 5 seconds instead of 20
> minutes. That's the story for Sarah in Peckham. She's not you —
> she's paying 7 percent, not 0.42."

*[You've made her the user of the interview, briefly. She'll
remember the point.]*

### Pushback B: "The regulatory risk seems very high to me."

**Wrong response:** "Let me tell you about our compliance…"

**Right response (agreeing then re-framing):**
> "I agree. Regulatory is the single biggest risk in this business
> — that's why 40% of the raise goes to it. But let me share the
> framing that made us confident enough to build it. UK Treasury's
> stablecoin consultation opened Q1 2026 — that's an active green
> light. MLR 2017 for CEP is a known-path licence. The FCA MTL
> process is 4-6 months for well-prepared applicants. We've built
> to standard from day one. So the risk is real, but it's addressable
> — and it's not novel. Wise, Revolut, Currencycloud all went
> through it."

### Pushback C: "This just feels too early / you're too small a team."

**Wrong response:** "But we've shipped so much…"

**Right response (acknowledge + specific counter):**
> "Yeah, we're early — that's why we're raising a seed and not a
> Series A. On team: two of us today, growing to five with this
> round. What we're optimising for at this stage is shipping speed
> and regulatory milestones. In the last six months, two of us
> shipped a full multi-chain remittance stack that would have taken
> a mid-stage startup twelve months. If you feel that specific
> execution rate isn't enough for your seed cheque, I'd love to know
> what you'd need to see."

*[This is a diagnostic question in disguise — you're forcing her to
articulate her actual objection.]*

## 🎬 THE LAST 60 SECONDS (0:19–0:20)

However well the call is going, you need to control the close.

### Script (roughly)

> "Julia, we're at 19 minutes and I know you have another call.
> Two quick things.
>
> One — what's your honest first read? I'd rather hear it directly
> than wonder for a week.
>
> Two — assuming there's fit, what's your usual process from here?"

**Why this works:**
- Direct honesty invitation → she has to give you a real signal
  (interested / meh / no)
- Asking about their process → forces them to name specifics (next
  meeting, partnership meeting, DD checklist)
- Time-awareness on the call → signals respect

### Handling the three possible responses

**Response 1 · "This is really interesting. Let's set up a follow-up."**
> "Wonderful. What week works? And who else on your side would you
> want to join? I'll send the deck + one-pager tonight."

**Response 2 · "I need to think about it / not sure it's a fit."**
> "Understood. If I could ask one favour: is there a specific
> question or concern that would move you from unsure to yes? If
> there is I'll bring you an answer. If it's a broader fund-thesis
> issue, that's also fine to hear."

*[This is a critical move. You're refusing to accept "maybe" without
diagnosis. Half the time she'll name the concern; the other half
you'll get a useful "no" that saves you both time.]*

**Response 3 · "Not a fit for us at this stage."**
> "Appreciated. Two things — one, would you be open to me sending
> you a quarterly update? And two, is there another fund on your
> radar whose thesis fits Vaulted better that I should be talking
> to?"

*[Both asks cost her nothing to say yes to. Update-lists convert 11%
of nos to yeses over 6 months. Names cost nothing to give — 40% of
the time you'll get one.]*

### The internal debrief (30 minutes)

Alone or with a co-founder, immediately after the call, write down:

1. **What worked?** (1-2 lines)
2. **What flopped?** (1-2 lines)
3. **What question did I fumble?** — this becomes the next drill
4. **What did she NOT ask?** — often more diagnostic than what she did
5. **Fit score: 1–10.** Adjust future outreach accordingly.

Do this after every VC call. By call 6 you'll be significantly
sharper than by call 1.

## 🎓 THREE PRINCIPLES TO INTERNALISE

### Principle 1: Frame is everything

You are not asking her to invest. You are both figuring out if this
is a fit. Every time you notice yourself getting supplicant, mentally
step back. Founders who project "please invest" get worse terms than
founders who project "I'm curious what you'd bring."

### Principle 2: The best answer to a hard question is a question

If she asks something you don't have a perfect answer to, buy time
with a genuine question back: "Interesting — how are you thinking
about that?" You're not deflecting; you're calibrating. The best VC
conversations are dialogues, not interviews.

### Principle 3: The call is not the decision

Even a "yes" on the 20-min call is just an invitation to the next
call. Even a "no" is a lesson you'll use on call 3. Play the meta-
game: what did I learn about how to describe Vaulted better? What
question needs a better answer? Every call feeds the next.

## ⚡ EMERGENCY MOVES

### Your video freezes / mic dies

Don't panic. Text her on WhatsApp or Slack immediately: "Video died
— restarting. Call you back in 90 seconds?" Then restart. She's
seen this a thousand times. Handling it calmly is a signal.

### She takes a call in the middle of yours

Wait patiently, camera on. Do NOT check your phone. When she comes
back: "No problem — where were we?" Continue as if uninterrupted.

### You blank on a stat

"I don't have that in front of me — I'll send it in the follow-up
tonight." Then move on. NEVER invent a number. If she catches an
invented number 3 weeks later, the round is dead.

### She pushes on a topic you know she's wrong about

"That's fair — my instinct is [counter-view] but I'd love to
understand what makes you feel that way." Curiosity beats defensiveness
every time.

### The whole call is going badly

Do the last-60-seconds close early. "I sense this may not be the
right fit right now — is there something specific I could sharpen
for a future conversation?" Ending gracefully at 15 minutes is 10×
better than dragging to 25.
