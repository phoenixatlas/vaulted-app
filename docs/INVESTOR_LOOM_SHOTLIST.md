# Vaulted — 60-second Investor Loom · Shot-list Script

**Purpose:** the single most important asset in your cold-outreach kit.
This Loom is what makes Julia at LocalGlobe actually click Play instead
of archiving. Do it well and reply-rate roughly doubles.

**Total runtime:** 60 seconds (hard cap — anything longer is a
courtesy watch). A 30-second LinkedIn variant is at the bottom.

**Format:** picture-in-picture. Founder webcam small in corner, screen
recording is the star. Voiceover throughout in your natural voice.

---

## Story architecture

| Beat | Duration | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Hook** (face + claim) | 0:00 – 0:04 | 4 seconds to earn the next 56 |
| **2. Problem** (specific person) | 0:04 – 0:12 | Human anchoring — Camden nurse, not "the diaspora" |
| **3. Demo** (screen recording, no cuts) | 0:12 – 0:38 | The wow — actual send happens on-screen |
| **4. Reveal** (chain explorer / audit log) | 0:38 – 0:48 | Proof the crypto rail actually settled |
| **5. Ask** (face + specific ask) | 0:48 – 0:60 | Convert the watch into a reply |

## THE SHOT-LIST (word-for-word)

### Beat 1 — Hook (0:00 – 0:04)

**On screen:** Founder face, warm eye contact, natural lighting.
Vaulted logo bug in bottom-right corner (subtle).

**Voiceover (spoken, not read):**
> "I'm going to send £50 from London to Nairobi in five seconds.
>  Watch."

**Delivery notes:**
- Say "five seconds" with a slight lift — this is the claim they'll remember
- Do NOT smile falsely — you're serious about this
- Close mouth after "Watch" — a full stop, not a trail-off

---

### Beat 2 — Problem (0:04 – 0:12)

**On screen:** Cut to a still overlay — a warmly-lit photo of an
NHS worker in London (royalty-free from Unsplash search: "NHS nurse
London" — pick one where the person is not identifiable). Small
text bar overlay: "Sarah, NHS nurse, Peckham → Nairobi".

**Voiceover:**
> "This is Sarah. Every month she sends £300 home to her mum.
>  Wise takes 22 pounds. That's a term of school fees."

**Delivery notes:**
- Say "22 pounds" slowly — that's the emotional pain point
- Substitute for a real user if you have one who's given permission,
  but the archetype works fine

---

### Beat 3 — The demo (0:12 – 0:38, 26 seconds)

**On screen:** Full-screen phone recording of Vaulted. Do this on
an actual phone with screen-recorder on (iOS: Control Centre →
Screen Recording; Android: Quick Settings → Screen Record). Then
import the .mov into Loom / import as B-roll.

**Actions on-screen, in this exact sequence:**

| Time | Screen action | Show for |
|---|---|---|
| 0:12 | App icon tap → wallet screen loads | 2s |
| 0:14 | Tap "Send Money" | 1s |
| 0:15 | Type "50" in the GBP input | 2s |
| 0:17 | Tap Kenya flag chip in country picker | 2s |
| 0:19 | Live quote appears: "£50 → 8,732 KES · fees £0.62 · ~5s" | 4s |
| 0:23 | Tap "Send" | 1s |
| 0:24 | Biometric prompt (Face ID / fingerprint icon) | 2s |
| 0:26 | Loading spinner (mimic 3s send delay if needed) | 3s |
| 0:29 | ✅ "Sent" success screen with receipt | 5s |
| 0:34 | Zoom in slightly on the receipt showing recipient's balance | 4s |

**Voiceover (overlaid on the screen recording):**

> (0:12) "She opens Vaulted."
>
> (0:15) "Types fifty pounds. Taps the Kenyan flag."
>
> (0:19) "The app quotes 8,732 shillings, fees 62 pence, arriving
>          in about five seconds."
>
> (0:23) "Face ID."
>
> (0:29) "Done. Her mum's phone just buzzed."

**Delivery notes:**
- NO cuts within the demo — one continuous screen recording earns trust
- Voiceover should be a couple beats BEHIND the action, so the viewer's
  eye lands on the screen first, ears catch up
- If the app is slow on any step, re-record — a stuttering demo kills the credibility

---

### Beat 4 — The reveal (0:38 – 0:48, 10 seconds)

**On screen:** Split screen — left half is a Polygonscan (or Solana
explorer, or Stellar explorer) showing the actual on-chain transaction
that just happened. Right half is the Vaulted audit-log JSON blob
(pretty-printed).

Highlight-box overlay on the transaction fee: "£0.001 gas".

**Voiceover:**
> "Under the hood that was USDC on Polygon. Chain cost: one tenth
>  of a penny. Vaulted's fee: sixty-two pence. No banks. No SWIFT.
>  And Sarah still owns her keys."

**Delivery notes:**
- The word "keys" at the end is what makes VCs sit forward — it's
  the structural differentiator. Land it clearly.
- Optional: pause 0.5s of silence after "still owns her keys" — the
  pause does the work of an exclamation mark

---

### Beat 5 — The ask (0:48 – 0:60, 12 seconds)

**On screen:** Cut back to founder face. Warm, direct. Bottom third
overlay with two text lines:
- "Vaulted — live in production · 6 chains · 8 corridors"
- "Raising £1.2M seed · Julia@localglobe / your-email"

**Voiceover:**
> "We're live in production. Six chains, eight corridors. Raising
>  a one-point-two-million seed to hit our FCA licence and twenty-
>  five thousand monthly users. If you back UK fintech, I'd love to
>  spend twenty minutes with you. Link in the email."

**Delivery notes:**
- Direct eye contact for the entire close
- Do NOT smile until "link in the email" — the smile earns you the
  reply, but only if the seriousness has landed first
- End with 0.5s of held eye contact before Loom stops recording. This
  is the frame the thumbnail preview will show — you want it warm.

## PRE-RECORDING SETUP CHECKLIST

Do this once, get it right, reuse the setup forever.

### Environment
- [ ] Quiet room. Not a café. No car noise, no fans running.
- [ ] Behind you: warm background — books, plant, textured wall.
      NOT a white bathroom door or a cluttered kitchen.
- [ ] In front of you: window light or a soft ring/panel light at
      45° angle. Front-lit face. **Never sit with a window behind you.**

### Camera
- [ ] Webcam at **eye level** — laptop-camera-below-your-face is
      the #1 amateur signal. Prop your laptop on a stack of books.
- [ ] Framing: your eyes should sit on the **top third** of the frame.
- [ ] Wear a solid colour top (dark navy, dark green, black). No
      logos, no patterns, no white.

### Audio (most important, most neglected)
- [ ] Do NOT use the laptop mic. Get a cheap USB lav mic (Rode Wireless
      Go II, £250) or borrow one. Bad audio kills more Looms than
      bad visuals.
- [ ] Test playback of a 10-second recording before you commit.
- [ ] If unavoidable and only laptop mic: sit within 30cm of the
      laptop, close the window, and turn every appliance off.

### App setup
- [ ] Loom Pro account (£12/mo) — free tier is time-capped
- [ ] Enable "Camera + Screen" recording mode with PIP
- [ ] Set video resolution to 1080p (NOT 720p — VCs may cast to a TV)
- [ ] Practice the demo three full times before you press Record
- [ ] Have the phone in **airplane mode** during screen recording so
      a notification banner doesn't appear on frame

### Content check
- [ ] Real KYC-verified test account so the send actually works
- [ ] Backend responsive (test the send flow in the last 60 minutes
      before recording — a slow API is death)
- [ ] Wallet balance sufficient for a £50 test send
- [ ] Recipient address (yours or a friend's) is on the whitelist so
      the send doesn't trigger any corridor block

## POST-PRODUCTION CHECKLIST

Loom lets you trim, add overlays, and add captions. Use all three.

- [ ] **Trim to exactly 60 seconds** — if you're at 62s, cut 2s from
      Beat 2. Never cut from Beat 3 (the demo).
- [ ] **Add auto-captions** — Loom's built-in captions. VCs watch on
      silent 60% of the time (Slack, in a meeting). No captions = no
      watch.
- [ ] **Add three text overlays**:
    - "£50 · UK → Kenya · 5 seconds · 62p fee" — during Beat 3
    - "USDC on Polygon · self-custody" — during Beat 4
    - "Vaulted · raising £1.2M seed · [your email]" — during Beat 5
- [ ] **Trim silences** — anything longer than 0.4s of dead audio
- [ ] **Enable password-off, viewable-by-anyone-with-link** — do NOT
      require Loom account
- [ ] **Custom thumbnail:** frame at 0:56 with your face + the £1.2M
      overlay visible. NOT the default "person mid-blink at 0:00".
- [ ] **CTA at end:** enable Loom's "Reply" button so viewer can
      voice-note back with one click

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Over-produced.** No stock music, no fancy transitions. VCs
   pattern-match "trying too hard" — a slightly rough Loom reads as
   authentic, a polished agency Loom reads as marketing spend.

2. **Under-produced audio.** See Audio checklist. This is the one
   place production quality DOES matter. Bad audio = 20% completion
   rate.

3. **Talking to the camera vs the audience.** You're not on TV.
   You're talking to Julia. Imagine her sitting across a table.
   Change nothing about the words, just the eye contact.

4. **The "and one more thing" sin.** Don't add a 10-second bonus at
   the end saying "oh and by the way we also do X." Cut it. This
   Loom does ONE job: get her to book the 20 minutes.

5. **The apologetic hedge.** "Obviously this is early…" or "sorry
   for the length…" — NEVER. Confidence is asymmetric: if you sound
   apologetic they discount you 50%; if you sound confident they
   discount you 10% for over-selling. Confidence is always the +EV
   choice.

6. **The demo failing on camera.** If the app hiccups during
   recording, STOP and re-record. Do not "explain around" a slow
   response. The rewatchability of the failure is worse than the
   extra 20 minutes of re-recording.

## RECORDING DAY PLAN (2 hours end-to-end)

If you block off Saturday morning:

* **09:00 – 09:20** — Setup: lighting, mic, camera, app rehearsal
* **09:20 – 09:35** — Do 3 full dry-runs of the script (out loud, no recording)
* **09:35 – 10:15** — Record 6–8 takes of the full 60s. Number them.
* **10:15 – 10:30** — Watch back. Pick your best full take, OR the best
  Beat 1+2, Beat 3, Beat 4+5 and stitch.
* **10:30 – 10:50** — Trim, add captions and overlays in Loom
* **10:50 – 11:00** — Add custom thumbnail, set sharing to
  "anyone with link", test in incognito

**By 11:00 you have the asset. By 11:15 you've sent it to Julia.**

Ship it.
